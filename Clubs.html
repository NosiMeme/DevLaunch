Clubs & internships

  • Girl Who Code: Girls Who Code Clubs are free after-school and weekend programs for girls in 3rd-12th grade, according to Girls Who Code. They are designed to provide a supportive environment for girls to explore coding and computer science, build community, and learn from inspiring role models. Clubs offer flexible curriculum, interactive activities, and opportunities for girls to make a positive impact on their communities through technology.

  • Robotic Club: A robotics club is a group of students, often part of a school or community organization, that comes together to learn about and work with robotics. They typically engage in activities like building, programming, and competing with robots, often focusing on hands-on projects and skill development.

  • Drone Club: We use the CoDrone EDU is a programmable drone for learning. Code it in Python or start from the basics with block-based coding. With its color sensors, a front range sensor, a bottom range sensor, and more, it's a great way to learn to code.

  • Techcorps : TECH CORPS is a nonprofit organization that provides K-12 students with access to Computer Science and Information Technology programs, skills, and resources. They aim to ensure equal access to these resources, enhance early learning, and prepare students for post-secondary education, credentials, or careers. TECH CORPS focuses on educating, exciting, and empowering students, as well as expanding program opportunities.
